Linen

Linen is a natural fabric made from the fibers of the flax plant. Known for its durability, breathability, and moisture-wicking properties, linen has a crisp texture and is often used in clothing, home textiles, and tableware. It wrinkles easily, which adds to its charm, giving it a relaxed, casual look.

Silk.

Silk is a luxurious, smooth fabric produced from the cocoons of silkworms. Renowned for its sheen and softness, it drapes beautifully and feels cool against the skin. Silk is often used in high-end clothing, bedding, and accessories, valued for its elegance and comfort.

Satin.

Satin is a glossy, smooth fabric that can be made from silk, polyester, or nylon. It has a soft texture and a luxurious appearance, often used in evening wear, lingerie, and home decor. Satin is known for its rich shine and elegant drape

Wool.

Wool is a natural fiber obtained from sheep and other animals. Known for its warmth, durability, and moisture-wicking properties, it is commonly used in clothing, blankets, and carpets. Wool is also breathable and can regulate temperature, making it versatile for various climates.

Cotton

Cotton is a soft, breathable fabric made from the fibers of the cotton plant. It's widely used in clothing, bed linens, and towels due to its comfort, versatility, and ease of care. Cotton is hypoallergenic and allows for good airflow, making it ideal for warm weather.

Denim

Denim is a sturdy cotton twill fabric, typically blue, known for its durability and versatility. Commonly used in jeans and jackets, it has a casual, rugged appeal and can be dressed up or down. Denim ages well, developing a unique character over time
